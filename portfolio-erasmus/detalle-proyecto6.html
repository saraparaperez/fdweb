Dressing Prejudice

Dressing Prejudice is a visual communication campaign born from the need to question how fashion—beyond being an aesthetic expression—can act as a social filter that shapes how individuals are perceived. In a society where appearances often define first impressions, this project reveals the deep connections between clothing, identity, and bias. The campaign investigates how a single garment can carry different connotations depending on who wears it and the context in which it appears. It draws from semiotics, visual rhetoric, and perception psychology to emotionally engage a young audience and spark critical reflection on the superficial judgments we make daily. In an era dominated by image, especially on social media, fashion becomes a code loaded with meanings—used to signal status, values, or credibility. Dressing Prejudice intervenes in this visual culture to question how these quick judgments fuel exclusion, stereotypes, and social pressure. It is a timely reflection on how young people are constantly exposed to image-driven environments that promote comparison and the pursuit of external validation.
